Valorant: A step into the tactical

  1. Valorant is a first-person tactical shooter character video game. And I absolutely love it.
    • Tactical shooter means players usually have low amounts of health, and the ttk, or time-to-kill is relatively small.
    • This means that the game becomes more skillful, as you can't simply charge into the enemy and expect to succeed.
    • That's why I like this game so much. It requires you to think on your feet. No two rounds are ever the same.
    • An additional feature is the diversity of characters. This is usually advertised in a fighting game, where a massive roster is preferred.
      But having that kind of diversity in a shooter makes the game all the more fun.

  1. With it's fast-paced gameplay, it's enough to challenge even veteran gamers with its speed of death.
    • Usual shooters like Halo or Call of Duty definitely have veteran players, as they have been out for decades at this point.
    • Valorant usually catches these veterans by suprise. With it's character roles and selection, it forces teams of 5 to play together, or lose.
    • There is less room for a lone wolf on these teams, and creates exciting plays where pieces of utility, or abilities, can be combined to outperform their competitors.
    • The roles of the different characters are as follows: Duelist, Controller, Initiator, and Sentinel.
  1. It balances tactical and strategic positioning, with raw talent and reflexes, leaving a place for everyone's playstyle.
    • In Valorant, you usually want to aim for the head, as this leads to the kill with most weapons in the game.
    • But for players who struggle with their aim (a.k.a. me), being in the right place on the map can net you the win.
    • This allows for players with varying areas of specialty to excel in their roles, and help their team to secure wins.
    • It becomes a game of 3D chess, and learning your opponent is almost as important as learning how to aim properly.
